No Solid State Triangular Lattice Hubbard Model?

This is a record of what Ashvin taught me during cookie time one day: why is it so hard to get triangular lattice models in solid state systems.

Assume that there is already a two-dimensional atomic triangular lattice, particularly with C3C_3C3​ symmetry. Note that Hubbard models usually arise out of d−d-d−wave orbitals: sss-orbitals are too close to the nucleus that the hopping ttt is too small (not sure what happens with ppp orbitals). To get a clean Hubbard model one can only have one orbital per atom. This means that we should study the singlet representations of C3C_3C3​ consisting of ddd-orbitals. Now:

  • dxzd_{xz}dxz​ and dyzd_{yz}dyz​ form a two-dimensional irrep of C3C_3C3​.

  • dx2−y2d_{x^2-y^2}dx2−y2​ and dxyd_{xy}dxy​ form a two-dimensional irrep of C3C_3C3​.

  • d2z2−x2−y2d_{2z^2-x^2-y^2}d2z2−x2−y2​ is a singlet under C3C_3C3​; however, its orbital shape is such that it does not have a large in-plane hopping ttt. This means that we also cannot construct a Hubbard model with these orbitals.

Hence we are not able to construct triangular lattice Hubbard models atomically. C4C_4C4​ evades this problem: dx2−y2d_{x^2-y^2}dx2−y2​ and dxyd_{xy}dxy​ are singlets under C4C_4C4​.
